Medical Lab Technician

Medical Lab Technicians are important members of any clinical laboratory team. Medical Lab Technicians analyze fluid, tissues and blood samples to perform a variety of diagnoses. Additionally, students from this course are trained to clean and maintain lab equipment, manage biomedical waste and adhere to quality control standards as per the NABL regulations. They generally work under the supervision of a pathologist. Course Curriculum

  • Pre & post-natal check up
  • Introduction to patient care, lab technology & equipment
  • Role of Medical Lab Technician
  • Basic pathology & diagnostic techniques
  • Basic & clinical biochemistry
  • Basic & systemic medical microbiology
  • Basic hematology, immunology, parasitology, histology, & general pharmacology
  • Blood banking, phlebotomy and handling special samples
  • Fine needle aspiration technique
  • Storage & transportation of samples
  • Lab information management system
  • Biomedical waste management
  • Infection control
  • Maintenance & cleaning of lab equipment
  • Personnel Hygiene and Safety & First Aid
  • Patient's Rights & Responsibilities
  • Professional Behavior in Healthcare Setting

    • Eligibility

    Duration

    : CMLT-1 year, DMLT- 2 Years

    Certification

    :- Bharat Sevak Samaj (promoted by Planning Commission, GOI)

    Internship

    : 6 months >

    Phisiotherapy

    E.C.G. Technician

    This course trains students in Emergency situation to detect whether the illness is due to Cardiac problem or else, Use of E.C.G. Machine of different makes & Models- Manual, Semi-Auto & Fully Auto, To carry out E.C.G. on pacemaker patient, Determination of heart beats, Axis, Cardiac Arrhythmias, Ventricular status, Interpretation of normal and abnormal E.C.G, Indication of E.C.G. recording, Requirement of Halter Study & Doppler study, TMT etc.

    General Duty Assistant (Nursing )

    This course trains students to work as nursing aides in hospitals and home care scenarios and they can also provide basic nursing care. They are taught to support nurses and also directly handle ill patients who are unable to look after themselves in both hospital and home care settings

    Course Curriculum

  • Basic anatomy & physiology
  • General health & hygiene
  • Understanding hospitals & the healthcare system
  • Special skin care for radiotherapy, pressure sores
  • Caring for the visually impaired
  • Role of a patient care assistant
  • Daily care for a patient
  • Administering drugs as per prescriptions
  • Basic nursing skills
  • Disposal of medical waste
  • Understanding body mechanics
  • Patient handling, lifting, and moving patients
  • Fall prevention care and restraints
  • Emergency first aid and CPR

    Minimum Qualification: 10th / Madhyamik passed. 10+2 passed is preferred. Minimum Age: 17 years. Maximum age: 30 years.

    Duration:

    1 year (Certificate) & 2 years (Diploma).

    Certification:

    Bharat Sevak Samaj (Promoted by Planning Commission, Govt. of India)

    Internship:-

    6 months

    Diet & Nutrition

    The focus of the programme is to enable you to make the best possible choice for meeting the nutritional needs of your family. The student will learn various concepts in nutrition, the role of nutrients such as carbohydrates, lipids, proteins, vitamins, and minerals, including their sources, requirements, functions, and roles in health. It also includes guidelines for nutrition, dietary reference intakes, dietary guidelines for Indians, and basics of nutritional assessment methods. 

    Curriculum:-

  • Introduction to nutrition
  • Macronutrients
  • Micronutrients
  • Nutritional Assessment
